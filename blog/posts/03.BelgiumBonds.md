# Belgium Government Bonds

## what is a bond? 
A bond is a debt instrument representing a loan extended by a creditor (the lender) to a debtor (the borrower, or *issuer*). The bond is held by the creditor; the issuer is obliged to repay the principal plus the interest — the *coupon* — owed on the debt. An entity in need of capital can raise it on the financial markets by issuing a bond. Prospective buyers assess the issuer's creditworthiness, and this determines the cost of the debt, or more precisely the interest the issuer must pay: the more creditworthy the issuer, the lower the interest demanded. Market conditions also affect the rate, since prevailing interest rates reflect the opportunity cost of capital — money lent to one borrower could have been deployed elsewhere. 

There are different types of bonds on the market and bonds are defined by their chracteristics. Below you can find an overview of the different chracateristic of  bonds: 

| Characteristic | Examples |
| -------- | -------- |
| Issuer | government, supranational, corporate, municipal, financial institution |
| Coupon structure | zero-coupon, fixed-rate, floating-rate; paid annually or semi-annually |
| Maturity | short, medium, long-dated, perpetual |
| Embedded options | callable, putable, convertible |
| Seniority / security | seniority — rank of the claim in the capital structure; security — whether the claim is backed by specific collateral |
| Principal / indexation | inflation-linked bonds (e.g. TIPS, OATi) |


## Bonds issued by the Belgium Government 

Measured by total value of securities outstanding, the global bond market is the largest securities market, exceeding the global equity market. I will therefore focus only on *Obligation Linéaire Ordinaire* / *Lineaire Obligatie* (OLO) of the Belgium Federal government. This is a fixed coupon, bullet maturity with no option bond (plain-vanilla).[^1] The description of the OLOs can be found on the [Belgium Debt Agency](https://www.debtagency.be/en/productoloinfo) (BDA). The OLOs are demateralized, meaning that there is no physical note linking to the bond. It purely lives on security account system managed by Clearstream and Euroclear. The name of the OLOs comes from the fact that a single bond is a line defined by a fixed coupon [^2] and fixed maturity date and one OLO is issued in successive tranches. Meaning that the outstanding debt from one OLO grows with each successive tranche.  This creates liquidity in the market as the capital is funneled into a few very large standardized lines which are actively traded.

The lineair property of the bond is derived by the fact that each line/OLO carries the nominal coupon rate and redemption date, regardless of which tranch or auction it came from. More precisily the contractual terms are identical accross tranchess and auctions, only prices will differ and mainly depends on market conditions. Another convenient property of this structure is fungibility. More precisily that all securities in one line/OLO are contractually identical and therefore any unit within the OLO is interchangable with any other.The last property of the OLOs is that its components (each coupon and the principal) can be seperated into their a zero-coupn security. This is called strippable securities or separate Trading of Registered Interest and Principal Securities (STRIP) and therefore you have coupon STRIP and a principal STRIP.

### Primary Market mechanics of the OLO 

The Belgium Debt Agency issues new OLO or tranches based on a predetermined [calander](https://www.debtagency.be/en/datafederalstateissues). On the calander you will find back the dates for the auctions of the OLOs. During an auction the Treasury reopens an existing line or creates a new one. The auction is open to primary dealers. Each dealer submits competitive bids where each bid is a pair; a *price* and a *quantity* they want to buy. Because the line is fixed the bidders only compete for the price of the OLO which maps directly to the yield. Each bid is ranked from highest price to lowest and the Treasury then fills from the top down untill it has sold its target amount. The lowest price still accepted is the stop price. Because the auction is a multiple-price auction, each bidder pays the price they themselves bid. The avereage price paid sits consequently above the stop price. Seperatly there are also primary dealers who have a privilege for a non-competitive subscription that lets them take on additional allotment at the weighted average price without bidding a specific price. ### Secondary Markets for OLO 

Once the Primary dealers have bought obligation from th auction, they trade these bonds on three secondary markets. The largest market by far is an over-the-counter (OTC) dealer market where transactions take place bilaterally like the MTS BElgium and BrokerTec. The second market are a business-to-customer trading like TradeWeb; BondVision and Bloomberg. The last market is a listed exchange market like the Euronext Brussels but is by far the smallest market from the three. 

Price setting is quote-driven which is different from the order driven equity markets most people are used to. In an order-driven equity market, a central limit order book matches anonymous buy and sell orders and the price is whatever the last match cleared at. In a quote-driven market, designated dealers (here, the OLO primary dealers) continuously quote a price at which they will buy (the bid) and a price at which they will sell (the ask), and you trade against the dealer's balance sheet rather than against another investor's order. The dealer warehouses the bond as inventory and earns the bid-ask spread.Liquidity on the OLO secondary market is ensured by the primary dealers as an obligation laid down in the specifications they sign with the Belgian State, which requires quoting negotiable interest rates or prices to their clients for the purchase and sale of OLOs, and quoting negotiable buy and sell prices on selected electronic platforms. The purpose is precisely to guarantee a price exists at all times
